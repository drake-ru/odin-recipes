Best Easy Pasta

Description

Disclaimer: none of the pictures used on my web pages are mine.

This is a very simple pasta recipe and a staple of mine - it is incredibly adaptable and uses ingredients that everyone will have hanging about in their kitchen cupboards.

The ingredients listed can be subbed out for similar ones, however the base for this is dry pasta and a tomato sauce. Quantities will be listed for a single portion, however they can scale up easily (as long as you have an appropriately-sized cooking pot for that).

Ingredients

  • 75g dry pasta
  • 125ml passata or half a tin of chopped tomatoes
  • 3 chesnut mushrooms
  • 1 spring onion
  • handful cherry tomatoes
  • marinaded beetroot (optional)
  • firm tofu (optional)
  • nutritional yeast (optional)
  • fresh rocket (optional)
  • salt and pepper to taste

Method

  1. Fill a medium-sized pot 3/4 full with freshly boiled water from the kettle and put it on a medium heat on the hob.
  2. Once the water is bubbling, throw in the dry pasta. Allow it to simmer (not aggressively boiling) for 8-10 mins, depending on how al dente you want your pasta.

  3. Whilst the pasta is cooking, prepare your ingredients:

    Chop the mushrooms, spring onion, cherry tomatoes and any other ingredients that can be chopped e.g. tofu and beetroot.

  4. Once the pasta has finished cooking, turn off the heat and drain the water. Return the pot to the lowest possible heat and add the passata/chopped tomatoes. Allow to simmer for 30s and then add the mushrooms (if you find them taking a long time to wilt, put a lid on top to cover so they steam faster, otherwise, if you're not bothered by lightly cooked mushrooms, don't bother).
  5. Add the remaining ingredients and stir well to coat in the sauce.
  6. Serve up and top with nutritional yeast, rocket and salt and pepper to taste, if so desired.
